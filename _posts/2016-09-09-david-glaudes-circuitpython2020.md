David from Belgium wrote [a GitHub Gist](https://gist.github.com/dglaude/9a9ca530f2a96d6087142fcc42ac40fa) with his perspective on CircuitPython in 2020. Check it out to hear how Micropython, BLE, and spoken languages can influence CircuitPython in 2020.

Here is a preview:

> I am David Glaude from Belgium and I am currently preparing a CircuitPython presentation for FOSDEM’20: [https://fosdem.org/2020/schedule/event/iotcircuitpython/](https://fosdem.org/2020/schedule/event/iotcircuitpython/)
> 
> In order to propose that presentation, I first worked on some demo I could present. Rather than to reinvent the wheel, I took some of the example code I could find around, picking stuff that could bring a WoW effect and that is based on BLE or Airlift.
> 
> Now that the presentation is accepted, I need to work on the slide and the story I want to tell. So I am forcing myself to consolidate my knowledge of CircuitPython history, release, hardware support, key decision, …
