**Gary Keith Starkweather** (born January 9, 1938, died December 26, 2019) was an American engineer and inventor most notable for the invention of the [laser printer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laser_printer "Laser printer") and [color management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_management "Color management").

Starkweather received a [B.S.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B.S. "B.S.") in [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics") from [Michigan State University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michigan_State_University "Michigan State University") in 1960 and an [M.S.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M.S. "M.S.") in [optics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optics "Optics") from the [University of Rochester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Rochester "University of Rochester") in 1966. In 1969, Starkweather invented the [laser printer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laser_printer "Laser printer") at the [Xerox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xerox "Xerox") Webster Research Center. He collaborated on the first fully functional laser printing system at [Xerox PARC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xerox_PARC "Xerox PARC") in 1971.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Starkweather#cite_note-1)[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Starkweather#cite_note-2)[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Starkweather#cite_note-3)

At [Apple Computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Computer "Apple Computer") in the 1990s, Starkweather invented [color management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_management "Color management") technology,[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Starkweather#cite_note-4) and led the development of [Colorsync](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colorsync "Colorsync") 1.0. In 1991 he was awarded the [David Richardson Medal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Richardson_Medal "David Richardson Medal").[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Starkweather#cite_note-5) Starkweather joined [Microsoft Research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Research "Microsoft Research") in 1997, where he worked on display technology.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Starkweather#cite_note-6)

In 2004, he was elected to the United States [National Academy of Engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Academy_of_Engineering "National Academy of Engineering").[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Starkweather#cite_note-7)
