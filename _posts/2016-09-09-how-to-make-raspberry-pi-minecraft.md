> It’s not super tricky to create a _Minecraft_ server on a [Raspberry Pi](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-raspberry-pi-4042337), though it does take some careful entry of text-based commands. While it won’t have the power of a larger home server or a _[Minecraft](https://www.lifewire.com/minecraft-realms-review-2363353)_ [Realm](https://www.lifewire.com/minecraft-realms-review-2363353), your Raspberry Pi _Minecraft_ server will let several people connect and play the popular game from their own devices. Multiplayer _Minecraft_ is a fun way to spend time with family and friends, whether you’re a kid or just a kid at heart.
> 
> First, though, let’s look at what hardware you’ll need to run your own _Minecraft_ server on a Raspberry Pi.
