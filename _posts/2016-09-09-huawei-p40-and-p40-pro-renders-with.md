![](https://images.fonearena.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/Huawei-P40-leak-1024x673.jpg) HUAWEI usually introduces its P series phones around March every year, now the company is gearing up to introduce its P40 series of phone in the next few months. Renders of the phone designed based on CAD drawings have surfaced showing off the phone's design and a large rectangular camera module for both the phones. The HUAWEI P40 is said to feature a 6.1 or 6.2-inch flat screen and the P40 Pro is rumored to come with a 6.5-inch OLED curved screen, probably with 120Hz refresh rate but it is not clear if the phones will feature a punch-hole display. The images show metal frame, USB Type-C ports, SIM card tray, and speaker grilles at the bottom. The volume rocker and power buttons are not the right, and the phones don't have a 3.5mm audio jack. These phones should be powered by Kirin 990 5G SoC and the company's CEO, Richard Yu has confirmed that it will run Android 10 based on EMUI 10 out of the box, but we can't expect Google apps until the Google issue is resolved. There are rumors of penta camera arrangement, including periscope telephoto lens and a ToF sensor for the Pro model, over 5000mAh battery ...
