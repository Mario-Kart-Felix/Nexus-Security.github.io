via [RaspberryPi.org](https://www.raspberrypi.org/blog/playback-your-favourite-records-with-plynth/)

> Plynth uses a Raspberry Pi and Pi Camera Module to identify cover artwork and play the respective album on your sound system, via your preferred streaming service or digital library.
> 
> As for Plynth’s innards? The stand houses a Raspberry Pi 3B+ and [Camera Module](https://www.raspberrypi.org/products/camera-module-v2/), and relies on “a combination of the [Google Vision API](https://cloud.google.com/vision/) and [OpenCV](https://opencv.org/), which is great because there’s a lot of documentation online for both of them”, states the project creator, [Jono Matusky](http://twitter.com/jonomatusky), on [Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/vinyl/comments/bz63qr/built_a_record_stand_that_uses_a_camera_to/).
