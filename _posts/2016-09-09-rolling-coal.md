**Rolling coal** is the practice of modifying a [diesel engine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diesel_engine "Diesel engine") to increase the amount of fuel entering the engine in order to emit large amounts of black or grey [sooty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soot "Soot") [exhaust fumes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diesel_exhaust "Diesel exhaust") into the air.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-July8-1) It also may include the intentional removal of the [particulate filter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diesel_particulate_filter "Diesel particulate filter").[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-2) Practitioners often additionally modify their vehicles by installing smoke switches and smoke stacks. Modifications to a vehicle to enable rolling coal may cost from US$200 to US$5,000.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-dahl-3)[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-4)

Background
----------

Rolling coal is a form of conspicuous [air pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution "Air pollution"), for entertainment or for protest.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-dahl-3) Some drivers intentionally trigger coal rolling in the presence of [hybrid vehicles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hybrid_vehicle "Hybrid vehicle") (when it is nicknamed "[Prius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toyota_Prius "Toyota Prius") repellent") to cause their drivers to lose sight of the road and inhale harmful air pollution. Coal rolling may also be directed at bicyclists, protesters, and pedestrians.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-5)[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-6)[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-7)[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-8) Practitioners cite "American freedom" and a stand against "rampant [environmentalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmentalism "Environmentalism")" as reasons for coal rolling.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-nyt2016-9)[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-10)

Health risks associated with rolling coal include respiratory issues. The [American Cancer Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Cancer_Society "American Cancer Society") has linked exposure to diesel exhaust to [lung cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lung_cancer "Lung cancer").[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-July8-1) A more [actionable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cause_of_action "Cause of action") concern is road traffic safety violations, as the black smoke can impair visibility, increasing the risks of motor vehicle crashes;[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-11) and violation of clean air laws.

Legality
--------

### United States

In July 2014, the [United States Environmental Protection Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency "United States Environmental Protection Agency") stated that the practice was illegal, as it violated the [Clean Air Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clean_Air_Act_(United_States) "Clean Air Act (United States)") which prohibits the manufacturing, sale, and installation "of a part for a motor vehicle that bypasses, defeats, or renders inoperative any emission control device \[and\] prohibits anyone from tampering with an emission control device on a motor vehicle by removing it or making it inoperable prior to or after the sale or delivery to the buyer."[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-12)[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-13) The New York Times published an article on rolling coal in September 2016.[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-14)

#### Colorado

Prohibits nuisance exhibition of motor vehicle exhaust, which is the knowing release of soot, smoke, or other particulate emissions from a motor vehicle with a gross vehicle weight rating of 14,000 pounds or less into the air and onto roadways, other motor vehicles, bicyclists, or pedestrians, in a manner that obstructs or obscures another person's view of the roadway, other users of the roadway, or a traffic control device or otherwise creates a hazard to a driver, bicyclist, or pedestrian.[\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-35)

#### Indiana

Vehicles must have equipment to prevent “escape of excessive fumes or smoke.”[\[36\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-36) A Reddit thread referenced the potential for issuing a citation based on creating a safety hazard by obscuring roadway visibility.[\[37\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-37)

#### Maryland

A person may not knowingly or intentionally cause a diesel–powered motor vehicle to discharge clearly visible smoke, soot, or other exhaust emissions onto another person or motor vehicle. Exempts normal operations, large commercial vehicles, and construction site vehicles.[\[38\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-38)

#### New Jersey

Prohibitions relative to certain retrofitting of diesel-powered vehicles. No person shall retrofit any diesel-powered vehicle with any device, smoke stack, or other equipment which enhances the vehicle's capacity to emit soot, smoke, or other particulate emissions, or shall purposely release significant quantities of soot, smoke, or other particulate emissions into the air and onto roadways and other vehicles while operating the vehicle, colloquially referred to as "coal rolling ."[\[39\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-39) New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection regulations also prohibit “smoking vehicles,” and the department has a reporting hotline.[\[40\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-40)

#### North Carolina

In 2016, a question to the Western North Carolina Air Quality Director about “rolling coal” referenced state law.[\[41\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-41) Vehicles driven on a highway must have equipment to prevent “annoying smoke and smoke screens.”[\[42\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-42) During any mode of operation, diesel-powered vehicles cannot emit for longer than five consecutive seconds visible contaminants darker than a specific density.[\[43\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-43)

Requires vehicle adjustment to “prevent the escape of excessive smoke or fumes.” Except when required to “maintain reasonable momentum,” vehicles cannot emit visible smoke for 10 seconds or longer, or visible smoke that “remains suspended in the air for 10 seconds or longer before fully dissipating.” Fine for a first offense up to $350, and $200-$1,000 for subsequent offenses.[\[44\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-44) County sheriffs have enforced the law.[\[45\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-45) The Texas Commission on Environmental Quality maintains a [website](https://www.tceq.texas.gov/airquality/mobilesource/vetech/smokingvehicles.html) to report vehicles with Texas license plates that emit excessive smoke.

Department of Motor Vehicles may suspend or revoke a vehicle’s registration if notified by a local health department that the vehicle is unable to meet state or local air emissions standards.[\[46\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-46) Except during warmup or heavy tow, or for vehicles with a gross vehicle weight greater than 26,000 pounds, a diesel engine may not emit visible contaminants during operation if manufactured after 2007, or may not emit contaminants greater than a specific density if manufactured before 2008.[\[47\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-47) An incident of coal rolling on a cyclist was captured on camera in August 2018 and referred to the Kane County attorney.[\[48\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-48)

### County or municipal ordinances and reporting

#### Hudson, Colorado[\[49\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-49)

It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in a nuisance exhibition of motor vehicle exhaust, which is the knowing release of soot, smoke, or other particulate emissions from a motor vehicle with a gross vehicle weight rating of fourteen thousand (14,000) pounds or less into the air and onto roadways, other motor vehicles, bicyclists, or pedestrians, in a manner that obstructs or obscures another person's view of the roadway, other users of the roadways, or a traffic control device or otherwise creates a hazard to a driver, bicyclist, or pedestrian. The ordinance, which was passed in September 2017, exempts several categories of vehicles, and provides for a fine up to $499.

The engine and power mechanism of every motor vehicle shall be so equipped and adjusted as to prevent the escape of excessive fumes or smoke.

#### Salt Lake, Davis, Utah, Weber/Morgan Counties, Utah

These counties have “smoking vehicle” report forms online.[\[52\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-52)

#### Cheyenne, Wyoming

A person shall not engage in a nuisance exhibition of motor vehicle exhaust, which is the knowing release of soot, smoke, or other particulate emissions from a motor vehicle with a gross vehicle weight rating of fourteen thousand (14,000) pounds or less into the air and onto roadways, other motor vehicles, bicyclists, or pedestrians, in a manner that obstructs or obscures another person's view of the roadway, other users of the roadway, or a traffic control device, or otherwise creates a hazard to a driver, bicyclist, or pedestrian.[\[53\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-53) The ordinance, which was passed in July 2017, exempts several categories of vehicles and provides for a fine of up to $750 and up to six months in jail.[\[54\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-54) A first attempt in July 2016 failed,[\[55\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-55) but Cheyenne police had clarified at that time that they had been writing tickets for coal rolling under state law.[\[56\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_coal#cite_note-56)
