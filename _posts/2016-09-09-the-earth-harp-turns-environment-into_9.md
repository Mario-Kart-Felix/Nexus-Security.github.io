> Have you ever seen an instrument that’s so large that the audience experiences it from inside the instrument? If not, then meet the Earth Harp, [an instrument that turns the environment into an integral component](https://www.williamearthharp.com/). It’s been named the “longest stringed instrument in the world” by Guinness World Records.
> 
> Invented by William Close about 20 years ago, each Earth Harp adapts to its surroundings. On its first incarnation Close ran strings about 1,000 feet from one side of a valley to the other, where four large resonators and bridge about the size of a 2 grand pianos were mounted to the ground.
