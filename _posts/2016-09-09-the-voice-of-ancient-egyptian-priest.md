> In what sounds like the premise for yet another reboot of _The Mummy_, engineers have synthesized the voice of a priest named Nesyamun who lived 3,000 years ago in the ancient Egyptian city of Thebes.
> 
> The results, [published in _Scientific Reports_](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-019-56316-y) on Thursday, include a short simulation of Nesyamun’s voice pronouncing a vowel sound. He honestly doesn’t sound thrilled to be vocally woken up after three millennia of silence.
