(Reuters) - Under Armour Inc on Monday cut its forecast for annual revenue for a second time this year, the latest sign of problems for the sportswear maker that is facing a federal probe into its accounting practices. Shares of the company closed about 19% lower after a media report that the company was being investigated for shifting sales from quarter to quarter to appear financially healthier. In a conference call with analysts, Under Armour executives defended the company's accounting practices and disclosures, and said they were cooperating with the federal investigators
